General Assembly

World Health Organization

"Health is a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity."

Committee Overview

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. The WHO Constitution, which establishes the agency's governing structure and principles, states its main objective as "the attainment by all peoples of the highest possible level of health."

In this committee, delegates will navigate complex medical ethics, distribution logistics, and international sovereignty to solve pressing global health crises.


Topic A: Mental Health in Conflict Zones

The Invisible Wounds of War

Populations in conflict-affected areas are vulnerable to severe psychological distress. Delegates must address the lack of mental health infrastructure in refugee camps, the stigma surrounding psychological trauma, and the integration of mental health support into standard humanitarian aid packages.

Topic B: Antibiotic Resistance

The Post-Antibiotic Era

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) threatens the effective prevention and treatment of an ever-increasing range of infections. This topic focuses on regulating antibiotic use in agriculture, incentivizing the development of new drugs by pharmaceutical giants, and ensuring equitable access to existing treatments in developing nations.
